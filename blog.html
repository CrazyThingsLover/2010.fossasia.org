FOSSASIA 2014 at Norton University in Phnom Penh, Cambodia from February 28 – March 2

Submitted by Hong Phuc on Wed, 13/11/2013 - 5:18pm

We are excited to announce FOSSASIA 2014 in Phnom Penh. After taking place several times in Vietnam, it is the first time FOSSASIA takes place in Cambodia. The event will be held at Norton University from February 28 – March 2, 2014.

FOSSASIA is a free event that offers students, developers and start ups a place to meet, share ideas and collaborate. It is a premier place for Open Source communities in Asia to get together with International developers and an opportunity to experience Open Source technologies.

Previous events attracted up to 1400 participants and speakers from 20 countries. Apart from Open Source Software FOSSASIA 2014 will feature a number of Open Hardware projects. In the FOSSASIA business lounge participants can learn about start up ideas and practices and connect with International companies and outsourcing partners.

Opera Software Banks on Android’s Rise for Speedy Growth

Submitted by admin on Thu, 20/06/2013 - 6:56am

With the rapid influx of low-cost smartphones from China, Asia is slowly becoming a hub for many smartphone brands. Market research firm GfK reported last month that from March 2012 to March 2013, the number of smartphones sold in Southeast Asia grew by 61%, pushing the smartphone sales in the region to the US$ 11 billion mark. Experts believe that the rise was due to the arrival of affordable phones and tablets, and high-end operating systems. When it comes to OS, most Asians prefer Android over other OS platforms. Android is having a sensational ride in the Asian market and one company that benefits greatly from the operating system’s success is Opera.

Opera Software senior vice-president for Asia, Fabrizio Caruso, announced last Tuesday (June 18) that Android just became the second largest mobile OS platform among Opera Mini users in many countries in Southeast Asia and India, marking a 160% growth of users in the region.

“From May 2012 to April 2013, we saw 148% Android Opera Mini user growth in India, 161% in Indonesia, 187% in Vietnam, and 214% in the Philippines,” Caruso said in an interview.

Web browsers play a critical role in a smartphone’s market success. Most smartphones, regardless of the continent, are now mainly used for browsing and other web-based features. People turn to their portable computers for emails, Google inquiries, games, and social media updates. Android phones are famous for their sleek interface and large developer community that create free applications. If they work on a reliable browser, there’s a bigger chance for that particular handset to be patronized by more consumers.

A reliable web browser is a must for various smartphone activities, especially when playing online games. These games include MMORPGs, online casino games, betting games, and first-person shooter games. Among all these, browser games like slots, roulette, and poker require high-end browsers for seamless and unparalleled gaming experience. While MMORPGs can only be played by installing certain software, most casino games can be played right on the browser. For instance, partycasino can be accessed without the need to download the entire program. The only thing players need to do is get their smartphones connected to the web and start playing. But before doing that, they need to make sure that their smartphones have a reliable web browser like Opera Mini to maximize the potential of games such as this.

Opera Mini should expect more as Android’s market growth shows no signs of slowing down. It is not just in Asia where the Linux-based operating system is dominant; it is also the most popular mobile OS in the United States and several European countries, as well. Apple’s iOS is far from second and Windows Phone is at third. Opera Software, as long as it runs on Android phones, sure has a promising future.

Digital Sewing Patterns Workshop with Susan Spencer

Submitted by Hong Phuc on Mon, 22/04/2013 - 8:12pm

The textil industry is dominated by some big players and proprietary file formats for digital cut patterns. With the maker scene getting a big push with 3D printers, it is time to push for open digital sewing pattern creation as well.

Susan Spencer already works for some time on this goal. She created plugins for creating and portraying sewing patterns in Inkscape and documents her work on the TauMeta Website.

The next goal is to enable designers to upload and customize patterns online through a web interface. This is why we are meeting up at a workshop after the Libre Graphics Meeting in Madrid. I believe this is exciting and offers many people a way to collaborate on patterns around the world.

As textil making is very big in Vietnam and other Asian countries, this could be very interesting for people. I can see that it would also offer ways for small business owners to work together.

Susan Spencer and Hong Phuc Dang at the Digital Pattern Workshop after LGM 2013

Crypto Stick accepted as Google Summer of Code Mentor Organization

Submitted by Hong Phuc on Thu, 11/04/2013 - 6:27pm

Crypto Stick has been accepted as a mentor organization for Google Summer of Code (GSOC) 2013. If you are a student interested in working on cool crypto stuff, developing a high security system, latest web security or embedded programming - this is for you. You can apply for a project and if accepted the mentors will work with you over the summer and Google will sponsor you 5000 USD during that time.

Please check out the ideas page at https://www.assembla.com/spaces/cryptostick/wiki/Ideas or suggest your own idea.

And, join the mailinglist to discuss it: https://lists.crypto-stick.org/mailman/listinfo/dev

Your applications should be submitted through Google Melange (https://google-melange.com) between March 26 and April 6. Generally, it is better to submit your application early as mentors like to discuss proposals.

Important dates are:

* March 17-25: Would-be student participants discuss application ideas with mentoring organizations.

* March 26, 19:00 UTC: Student application period opens.

* April 6, 19:00 UTC: Student application deadline.

You can continue to submit additional information and comments into the system after your initial application submission. In order to participate in the program, you must be a student in an accredited institution or university.
